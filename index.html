The Future of Mobility is Everyday Wearables

Introducing Now 1.0, the world's first consumer-grade mobility exoskeleton designed for everyday life, not clinical, industrial or athletics use.

We build lightweight, AI-powered wearables that support walking and daily tasks, helping adults 45+ stay active and independent without medical devices or complex screens.

Unlike clinical or industrial exoskeletons, Now 1.0 is designed for the city. It combines soft robotics with on-device sensing and adaptive voice guidance to make every step safer and easier.

Features of Now 1.0

Powered Mobility Assist

Lightweight, soft, battery-powered exoskeleton for mobility assist.

Environmental Sensing

LiDAR sensing for obstacle detection and haptic feedback system for alerts to avoid collisions.

Body Vitals Tracking

Waistbelt device tracks heart rate, oxygen levels and gait patterns.

Fall Alert System

Now 1.0 detects falls and automates caregiver alert through calls and messages.

AI Capability

On-device AI Processor computes data and generates live, moment-to-moment voice coaching for safe, guided mobility experience. It also contacts emergency numbers in case of falls.

Ageing is a Mobility Challenge

Evidence suggests that early mobility decline begins in the 40s, with gradual worsening through the 50s, 60s, and 70s. Older adults withdraw from social and public life. Mobility decline leads to cognitive decline, isolation, depression, and overall health decline.

At NOW, we beileve that addressing early mobility challenge is key to active ageing and happy older life.

About

NOW

Now is an active-ageing wearables company designing soft, AI-powered exoskeletons for everyday walking and other physical tasks like lifting, pushing, and pulling, for active older adults.

Now 1.0 is our flagship mobility assist wearable and Grip 1.0 is our next-in-line upper-limb wearable to perform everyday tasks using your hands.

Our core ideology is - Ageing is a mobility problem, not a clinical problem. Our products are designed to help older adults with enhanced participation in everyday life. Just "Put in on and walk".
